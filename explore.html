Academic Integrity Guide

1. Understanding Academic Integrity

Academic integrity is the commitment to and demonstration of honest and moral behavior in an academic setting. This is most relevant at the university level and is a fundamental value in teaching, learning, and research.

2. Types of Examination Misconduct

Examination misconduct includes various forms of cheating, such as copying, using unauthorized materials, impersonation, and collusion. It's crucial to understand these types to avoid unintentional violations.

3. Plagiarism and How to Avoid It

Plagiarism is the act of using someone else's work or ideas without proper attribution. Learn about proper citation methods, paraphrasing techniques, and tools to check for unintentional plagiarism.

4. Consequences of Cheating

Cheating can have severe consequences, including failing grades, suspension, expulsion, and long-term impacts on your academic and professional career. Understanding these consequences reinforces the importance of academic integrity.
